# Founder Response — to Thabo

Hi Thabo, thanks for the brief. Here's how I approached the sprint and the calls
I made.

## What I built this sprint (and why these earned the spot)

- I have decided to go with the main points that will focus on making sure that the app gives you functionality and costumer satisfaction. I have included browse section as it is vital for such a product and also kept search and filter, item detail and booking but booking with real auth as it serves as a legitimate and makes sure that the people booking are verified.

## What I cut or deferred (and why)

1. I have decided to cut out the following:
   Forced signup before viewing anything — Holding back all content behind signup tanks conversion looks like a lead-gen trap, not a marketplace. Better you can let people browse freely, gate only the action (booking, messaging, listing an item) behind auth.This way you still capture emails, just at the point of intent, which converts far better.

2. "3 people are looking at this right now!!" — unless it's true. Fabricated urgency counters are a classic dark pattern and a fast way to lose trust the first time a user senses it's fake. If you want real signal later, show real activity (e.g. actual watch count, actual recent bookings) once you have volume — never a hardcoded/randomized number.

3. "Make it look busy even with no users" — for the same reason. Faking inventory/activity is deceptive and creates a cliff the moment someone in a small neighbourhood realizes the listings aren't real. Therefor designing the empty/low-density state to look intentional (e.g. "Be the first to list a tool in your area" + nearby-area expansion), not fake it.

## What I pushed back on (and why — be honest and kind)

"Better than Airbnb", "INSANE", "premium" — translated into an actual design requirement below rather than left as a vibe.
